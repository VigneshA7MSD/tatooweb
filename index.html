We work the way an iguana does — still for a long time, then exact.

Iguana Tattoo opened as a two-chair studio for people who wanted their tattoo considered before it was inked — a design worth wearing for the next forty years, not the next four months.

Every consult starts with a sketch and a conversation, not a form. We work in fine line, blackwork, traditional color, and photo-real portraiture, and we'll tell you honestly which one suits what you're asking for.

The studio is small on purpose. Fewer chairs, more attention, one artist per piece from first pencil to final wipe-down.

How a session runs

Four sittings,
one piece.

01

Consult

Twenty minutes with your artist. Bring references, skin, and an honest sense of scale and placement.

02

Design

A custom stencil is drawn to your body, not lifted from a printer. You approve it before ink touches skin.

03

Ink

Sessions run 2–5 hours depending on size. Breaks are built in — we're not racing the outline.

04

Heal

Aftercare instructions, a wrap that actually works, and a check-in message at the two-week mark.
